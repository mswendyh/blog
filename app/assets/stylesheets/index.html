Bio

I am the Digital Collections Archivist at the Georgia Tech Archives. I received my M.S.I.S. with a focus on digital archives and preservation from the University of Texas at Austin School of Information. My areas of scholarly and professional interest include personal digital archiving as an outreach and advocacy tool for increasing awareness about the importance of digital archiving in society, the technological and intellectual property challenges of preserving and providing access to born-digital data and proprietary formats, and the puzzles of data stewardship and control in an increasingly terms-of-service-bound world. I love Goldfish crackers.
